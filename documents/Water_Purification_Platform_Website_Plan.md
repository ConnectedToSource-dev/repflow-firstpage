# Water Purification Platform — Website & Funnel Strategy Plan

## 1. Platform Overview & Positioning

Your platform sits at the intersection of two massive, growing markets: the door-to-door home services sales industry (solar, pest control) and the residential water purification market, which is projected to reach $91B+ globally by 2034. The core thesis is elegant: **solar reps and pest control companies already have the homeowner's trust and attention — give them an additional high-commission product to sell, and connect them with a network of certified installers who fulfill the orders.**

**Platform name placeholder:** [Your Brand] — *The marketplace that connects sales reps, water purification systems, and certified installers.*

**The three-page website architecture:**

| Page | Purpose | Audience |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Main Homepage** | Platform overview, credibility, ICP routing | Both audiences + general visitors |
| **Sales Rep Funnel Page** | Convert solar/pest control companies | Companies with door-to-door reps |
| **Installer Funnel Page** | Convert water purification installers | Existing water system installers |

## 2. ICP #1: Solar Reps & Pest Control Companies

### 2.1 Who They Are

These are companies (not individual reps) that employ door-to-door sales teams selling residential solar panels, pest control contracts, or similar home services. They already have:

- Trained sales teams knocking on doors daily
- Existing homeowner relationships and trust
- A proven sales process and territory coverage
- Infrastructure for commission tracking and payouts
- Motivation to increase revenue per door knocked

**Company profile:** 5–200+ reps, operating in residential territories across the US, commission-based compensation structures, always looking for add-on revenue streams to increase per-rep profitability and reduce turnover.

### 2.2 Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)

**USP 1: "$3,000+ Extra Commission Per Sale — No New Doors Required"**
Solar reps already earn $1,000–$2,500 per solar deal. Adding a water purification upsell means an additional $3,000 in commission from the same household visit. This is pure incremental revenue — no additional lead cost, no additional door knocking.

**USP 2: "Sell-Ready in Under 1 Hour"**
Your platform provides everything needed to start selling: training materials, product knowledge, sales scripts, pricing tools, and a digital system to submit orders. Reps don't need to become water purification experts — the platform handles the complexity.

**USP 3: "Zero Fulfillment Headaches — We Handle Installation"**
The rep sells, the platform matches with a local certified installer, the installer fulfills. The rep never has to worry about scheduling, inventory, permits, or service calls. Sell it and move on to the next door.

**USP 4: "Your Reps Are Already Talking About Water Quality"**
Solar reps are already inside the home discussing energy savings and home improvement. The conversation about water quality is a natural extension — especially with growing consumer awareness about PFAS, lead, chlorine, and microplastics in tap water.

**USP 5: "Reduce Rep Turnover — Give Them More Ways to Earn"**
The solar industry suffers from high rep turnover. Reps constantly jump to companies offering better commission structures. By offering an additional $3,000 per-sale opportunity, your company becomes stickier — reps earn more and stay longer.

### 2.3 Objection Handling Matrix

| Objection | Root Fear | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| "My reps don't know anything about water purification" | Fear of looking incompetent at the door | Emphasize 1-hour onboarding, script-based selling, and that the platform handles all technical questions post-sale |
| "This will distract from our core solar/pest control sales" | Fear of cannibalization | Position as an upsell AFTER the primary sale is closed — it adds to the visit, doesn't replace it |
| "How do I know the installation quality won't hurt my reputation?" | Brand risk | Highlight your installer vetting process, quality standards, warranties, and customer satisfaction tracking |
| "What if the customer has a problem after installation?" | Liability concerns | Clarify that the platform and installer handle all post-sale support — the rep's involvement ends at the sale |
| "The commission sounds too good to be true" | Skepticism/scam detection | Show transparent economics: a residential water purification system sells for $3,000–$8,000, making $3,000 in rep commission viable within the margin structure |
| "We've tried add-ons before and they didn't work" | Past failure baggage | Differentiate: water purification has a natural conversational fit with home visits, unlike unrelated products. Plus, your platform eliminates the operational burden they had before |
| "I don't want to manage another vendor relationship" | Operational complexity | The platform IS the vendor — one dashboard, one relationship, one payout system |

### 2.4 What They Need to See on the Website

- **Income calculator**: "If your 20 reps each close 2 water systems per month = $120,000/month in additional commissions"
- **Speed-to-revenue**: Visual timeline showing "Sign up → Train → First sale" in under a week
- **Software demo/screenshots**: Dashboard showing lead tracking, commission payouts, installer matching
- **Social proof**: Testimonials from other sales companies already on the platform
- **No-risk framing**: No upfront costs, no inventory, no commitment — interest-based sign-up
- **Competitive positioning**: "Your reps are already inside the home. Don't leave $3,000 on the table."

## 3. ICP #2: Water Purification Installers & Fulfillers

### 3.1 Who They Are

These are existing plumbing companies, water treatment specialists, and independent installers who already install residential water purification systems (reverse osmosis, whole-house filtration, water softeners, UV purification). They typically:

- Have licensed technicians and service vehicles
- Already purchase and install water treatment hardware
- Spend significant money on marketing and lead generation (Google Ads, HomeAdvisor, Angi, etc.)
- Operate in defined service territories
- Have variable workload — some weeks full, some weeks slow
- Sell systems ranging from $1,000–$8,000+ per installation

**Company profile:** 1–50 technicians, local/regional service area, revenue dependent on lead flow, established reputation but growth constrained by customer acquisition cost.

### 3.2 Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)

**USP 1: "Endless Leads, Zero Lead Cost"**
Installers currently pay $50–$250+ per lead through digital advertising, lead aggregators, and referral platforms. Your platform delivers pre-sold, installation-ready leads directly to them — at no acquisition cost. The sales rep has already closed the homeowner.

**USP 2: "Same Price You'd Normally Charge — No Revenue Loss"**
Unlike lead aggregators that compress margins, your platform lets installers charge their standard installation rates. The economics work because the sales cost has already been absorbed by the rep's commission structure.

**USP 3: "Fill Your Calendar — Eliminate Slow Weeks"**
Water purification installers often have unpredictable workloads. The platform provides a steady stream of installation jobs to fill gaps, smooth revenue, and keep technicians productive.

**USP 4: "Pre-Qualified, Pre-Sold Homeowners"**
These aren't cold leads. A trained sales rep has already been inside the home, explained the benefits, and closed the sale. The installer shows up to a homeowner who is expecting them, has agreed to the system, and is ready for installation.

**USP 5: "Grow Without Growing Your Marketing Budget"**
Every dollar an installer currently spends on Google Ads, Angi, HomeAdvisor, or mailers is a dollar at risk. Platform leads are incremental — they come on top of existing marketing efforts, not instead of them.

### 3.3 Objection Handling Matrix

| Objection | Root Fear | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| "I don't trust that a solar rep can sell water systems properly" | Quality of the sale / customer expectations | Explain the training program, standardized product offerings, and that the installer can verify specifications before committing to the job |
| "What if the customer was oversold or has wrong expectations?" | Callback risk, warranty disputes | Platform includes a verification step between sale and installation, plus clear scope documentation |
| "I already have enough leads from my own marketing" | No perceived need | Position as supplemental, not replacement. "Even if you're full now, what about slow months? Plus, these leads cost you nothing." |
| "What's the catch? Why are the leads free?" | Suspicion of hidden costs | Transparent explanation: the sales rep earns commission from the sale price margin, the installer earns their standard installation fee — the platform takes a platform fee that's already built into the pricing |
| "I don't want to install brands/products I don't know" | Product quality concerns | Clarify that installers can work with approved product lines they're already familiar with, or the platform's vetted product catalog |
| "What about my service area? I only work in [region]" | Geographic mismatch | Explain that the platform matches leads by service area — they only receive jobs within their defined territory |
| "What if I'm too busy to take on more work?" | Capacity constraints | Installers control their availability in the dashboard. Toggle on/off, set maximum jobs per week, pause when needed |

### 3.4 What They Need to See on the Website

- **Lead volume indicator**: "X installers in your area are already receiving Y leads per month"
- **Revenue calculator**: "At your average installation price of $4,500 — 10 additional installs per month = $45,000 in additional revenue"
- **Zero-cost emphasis**: Giant, unmissable messaging that these leads cost them nothing
- **Software demo/screenshots**: Dashboard showing incoming leads, job scheduling, territory map, payment tracking
- **Quality assurance**: How the platform vets sales reps and ensures proper customer expectations
- **Flexibility messaging**: "You choose your schedule, your territory, your capacity"
- **Testimonials**: Other installers talking about filled calendars and predictable revenue

## 4. Software Platform — What to Show on All Pages

The software is the backbone of your credibility. Both ICPs need to see that this isn't just a promise — it's a real, functional system. Here's what the software sections should communicate:

### 4.1 For Both Audiences (Homepage)

**Dashboard Overview**
Show a polished, realistic software interface with:
- Clean navigation showing separate portals for reps and installers
- Real-time metrics (leads in pipeline, installations scheduled, commissions earned)
- Map view showing active territories, installer coverage, and lead density
- Notification system for new matches, completed installations, and payouts

**Key Platform Features to Highlight:**

- **Matching Engine**: Automatically connects a closed sale with the nearest qualified installer
- **Commission Tracking**: Real-time visibility into earnings, payouts, and performance
- **Job Management**: Full lifecycle from sale → verification → scheduling → installation → completion
- **Territory Management**: Define and manage service areas for both reps and installers
- **Training Hub**: Onboarding materials, product knowledge, sales scripts (for reps)
- **Quality Control**: Customer feedback loop, installation verification, dispute resolution
- **Reporting & Analytics**: Performance dashboards for company owners and managers

### 4.2 For Sales Reps (Funnel Page)

Show software screens focused on:
- Quick-entry sale submission form (capture customer info, system selected, price)
- Commission tracker with pending, confirmed, and paid columns
- Sales leaderboard (if managing a team)
- Training module progress tracker
- Product catalog with pricing and feature comparisons

### 4.3 For Installers (Funnel Page)

Show software screens focused on:
- Incoming lead feed with customer details, system specs, and location
- Accept/decline interface with job details
- Calendar integration showing scheduled installations
- Payment tracking and invoice history
- Territory map with active and available zones
- Availability toggle and capacity settings

## 8. Design & Brand Direction

### 8.1 Visual Identity Recommendations

- **Primary palette:** Deep navy/dark blue (trust, professionalism) + electric teal or bright cyan (innovation, water/purity association) + white space
- **Accent:** Warm gold or amber for CTAs and highlights (premium, commission/money association)
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif for headings (something with character — not Inter or Roboto), clean readable body font
- **Photography direction:** Real people in home environments, installation shots, happy homeowners, sales reps in action — avoid stock photo clichés
- **Iconography:** Custom line icons for the process steps, software features, and USPs

### 8.2 Tone of Voice

- **Confident but not salesy** — you're building a platform, not running a pitch
- **Direct and transparent** — especially about money, commissions, and how the economics work
- **Professional with energy** — you're talking to business owners and operators, not consumers
- **Proof-driven** — lead with numbers, data, and specifics over vague promises

### 8.3 Mobile-First Approach

Both ICPs (field sales managers and local installers) are heavily mobile. The site must be:
- Fully responsive with mobile-optimized forms
- Fast-loading (these are busy people on the go)
- Thumb-friendly CTAs and navigation
- Forms that work seamlessly on phones

## 9. Technical & Functional Requirements

### 9.1 Interest Form Backend

Since onboarding is manual and selective:
- Forms should submit to a CRM or database (Airtable, Notion, HubSpot, or custom)
- Auto-tag submissions by ICP type (Rep Company vs. Installer)
- Auto-send confirmation email upon submission
- Internal notification to your team for review
- No auto-approval messaging — keep it vague: "Thank you for your interest. Our team will review your application and be in touch within 48 hours."

### 9.2 Analytics & Tracking

- UTM parameter support for all pages (you'll run ads or share links to specific funnels)
- Event tracking on: page views, CTA clicks, form starts, form completions
- Heatmapping (Hotjar or similar) to optimize conversion
- Separate conversion tracking for each funnel page

### 9.3 SEO Foundations

- Unique meta titles and descriptions for each page
- Schema markup for the organization
- Fast page speed (< 3 seconds load time)
- Blog/content section for future SEO expansion (articles about water quality, sales tips, installer business growth)

## 11. Key Metrics to Track Post-Launch

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Homepage → Funnel click-through rate | > 25% | Are visitors self-identifying and routing? |
| Funnel page → Form start rate | > 15% | Is the page compelling enough to begin the form? |
| Form start → Form completion rate | > 60% | Is the form too long or asking wrong questions? |
| Rep applications per week | 10+ | Pipeline health for rep side |
| Installer applications per week | 10+ | Pipeline health for installer side |
| Qualified rate (applications that meet criteria) | > 40% | Is the messaging attracting the right people? |
| Time on page (funnel pages) | > 2 minutes | Are people actually reading the content? |
